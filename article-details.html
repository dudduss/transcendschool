Mortgage Loan Officer: An Overview

What is a Mortgage Loan Officer (MLO)?

An MLO qualifies prospective homeowners for a home loan. These professionals tend to work closely with realtors to help homebuyers purchase a home while having a monthly payment that fits within their budget. After this transaction, a mortgage loan officer can assist with lowering the homeowner’s mortgage payment with a refinance or taking equity out for various reasons such as personal debt consolidation, home improvements and other investments. For reference, this position is also called “Mortgage Broker” or “Mortgage Loan Originator”.

What does the day to day of an MLO look like?

An MLO’s day is filled with conducting phone and video calls with new, current, and repeat clients along with meeting with realtors and trusted advisors (CPAs, trust attorneys, and financial advisors). In between the calls and meetings, MLOs communicate with processors and underwriters to determine next steps needed to complete the purchase or refinance transaction for their clients.

Why should I become a MLO?

The MLO job is ranked #8 in the U.S News & World Report’s rankings for best business jobs , with a median salary of $63,000. This job is appealing because it doesn’t require education beyond a high school diploma. It also offers the ability to work from home and lots of exciting career advancement opportunities.

What do I need to become an MLO?

You will need to pass the NMLS SAFE test and fulfill the 20-hour pre-licensing education requirements set by federal guidelines. Depending on the state you want to conduct business, you may need to take additional pre-licensing education hours. You will also need to develop your sales skills to communicate effectively with prospective borrowers, educate them on their loan options, and convince them to do business with you. To grow your business, you will also need to learn how to create a network of realtors and a robust referral system. Luckily, Transcend teaches you all of that and more!

Where can I work as an MLO?

MLOs work at non-bank direct lenders, brokerages, or depository banks. Transcend will guide you with understanding which employer option is best for you to succeed!
