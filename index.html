FAQ

What is a Mortgage Loan Officer (MLO)?
An MLO is an individual that helps a prospective borrower get the right mortgage for a real estate transaction (i.e. mortgage, refinance). The MLO is the original lender for the mortgage and works with the borrower from application and approval through the closing process. Other names for this job are "mortgage broker" and "mortage loan originator".
How can I make $100,000 a year as an MLO?
Our employer partners have pay structures that are commission-focused, typically paying 1-2.5% of a loan amount. Considering an average home loan amount, you'd have to originate 3-4 loans a month to make $100,000 annually. We believe our training will not only help get you there, but far exceed that in just a few months on the job.
How much do I have to pay Transcend?
Your repayments are tied to your income. You will pay us 10% of your of your monthly pre-tax income until you've paid back a total of $20,000. However, you only repay us if your annual income is over $100,000 per year (or $8,333 per month).
What happens if I stop making more than $100,000 a year?
Your account will be placed in deferment if you can demonstrate that you are earning less than $100,000 a year or are unemployed. We will extend your payment term by one month for each month of deferment, up to 36 months. You can also elect to pay the remaining balance.
When do I stop paying Transcend?
Either when you have paid back the full $20,000 or you've accumulated 60 total months of deferment and repayment, regardless of how much you've paid up until that point. We don't want you to indebted to us forever.
How does Transcend know how much money I will make?
The payments will be managed by a third party partner called Blair. Blair provides an online portal for bank account and direct deposit verifications that allow for payments to get calculated and collected.
Do I have to take a job with a Transcend employer partner?
Nope! You have the full discretion to reject any job offer that you get through our partner employers and look elsewhere.
What happens if I don’t get a job with a Transcend employer partner?
We'll try our best to make sure you get matched. In the unlikely case that it doesn't happen, we will help you apply/refer you to open jobs and guide you throughout the interview process until you land a job.
